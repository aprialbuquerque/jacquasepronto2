Rio de Janeiro is home to some of the best forró and samba de gafieira venues in Brazil.



Forró Dancing

Forró is a popular Brazilian dance style characterized by its lively rhythms and close partner dancing. Here are some places where you can dance forró in Rio:

  • Restaurante Vuvu: Located at Rua Sorocaba, 601, Botafogo, Rio de Janeiro, Restaurante Vuvu hosts forró nights on Tuesdays. Join the fun and dance the night away!
  • Praça da São Salvador: Every Friday night, Praça da São Salvador comes alive with forró dancing. Head over to this vibrant square for an unforgettable dance experience.


Forró Classes

If you're looking to learn forró and Samba, there are several great options for classes in Rio de Janeiro:

  • Jaime Aroxa Copacabana: Located at Avenida Nossa Senhora de Copacabana, 1138, Jaime Aroxa Copacabana offers excellent forró classes. With air conditioning, a fantastic dance floor, and a prime location in Copacabana, Rio's most tourist-friendly neighborhood, it's the perfect place to learn this exciting dance style.


Don't miss out on the opportunity to experience the joy of forró and samba de gafieira while you're in Rio de Janeiro. Whether you're dancing or learning, Rio has something for everyone!

Luiz Bragança

Luiz Bragança: Forró teacher, avid anime and cinema enthusiast.

Luiz Bragança has had a solid and long career in dance, which began when he was a teenager. Over the years, he has traveled and lived in various parts of the world, teaching Forró, samba de Gafieira, and other rhythms. His seasons included stints in Egypt, Switzerland, Italy, and various regions of Europe.

Additionally, Luiz Bragança speaks English and French fluently, and he loves teaching tourists due to the cultural and linguistic diversity.

Currently, Luiz teaches group and private classes at the Jaime Arôxa Copacabana Dance School.
